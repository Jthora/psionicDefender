# Critique and Findings

## Executive summary
The current wiki psionics corpus is strongest when it maps to known electromagnetic, acoustic, and neurostimulation effects, and weakest when it presents unvalidated scalar/field equations as settled physical theory.

Main issue: the text frequently blends three layers without strict boundaries:
1. Established physics/biomedicine (Frey effect context, TMS/tDCS class effects, EMI principles).
2. Patent existence and historical programs (which show interest, not proven deployable systems).
3. Narrative field-theory constructs (Psi scalar potentials, stress-energy tensors, and fifth-force terms).

## What appears technically credible
- Directional RF and pulsed RF effects are plausible in principle and should be treated as a threat model input.
- Electronic warfare style jamming/deception concepts are valid analogies for sensor and comms disruption.
- Layered defense architecture is a strong engineering pattern: detect, filter, isolate, shield, recover.

## What is weak or over-claimed
- Patent presence is used as proof of operational capability.
- Many equations define symbols but do not define measurable units, calibration protocol, or falsification criteria.
- Scalar-field claims are presented as if experimentally grounded when they are not currently supported by mainstream reproducible evidence.

## Practical interpretation for engineering
Use a five-channel threat model instead of a single "psionic" bucket:
1. RF/microwave pulsed exposure.
2. Electric/magnetic near-field coupling.
3. Acoustic (infrasound through ultrasound) and vibration.
4. Conducted EMI via power and cabling.
5. Information operations and perception-level attack patterns.

This model is measurable with available hardware and does not depend on accepting unverified ontology.

## Safety-critical note
Very high voltage modules (400 kV and 1 MV class) are not required for defensive validation and should not be used in body-worn prototypes.

## Recommended wiki quality upgrades (next pass)
- Add claim tags: {established, plausible, speculative, narrative}.
- Add per-claim source lines and confidence level.
- Add "how to falsify" section for each equation-heavy page.
- Add unit tests for equations: dimensions, variable definitions, and expected sensor observables.
